Every perspective has some kind of bias.
Click the AREA Method steps below to see the different kinds of bias in each perspective.

Absolute

Absolute information is primary source information from the targets of your decision, it's the least influenced information, having the bias of the targets alone. For Nylah that would be Ohio State and Bates.

Relative

Relative sources are sources that are somehow connected - related - to your research target, but are not from the target itself, such as news media or industry groups and so there is an extra layer of bias embedded in it from those secondary sources.
