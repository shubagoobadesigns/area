Building Conviction: How Could Your Critical Concepts Fail?

Although your vision of success may not change as you solve your decision problem, your Critical Concepts may change --and they should--as you collect new information and gain new insight.

To build conviction that you’ve nailed your starting CC’s, test them out: For each Critical Concept, pretend that it “fails.”

The following describes how Nylah tackled her decision Critical Concepts: "Should I go away to college?"

Nylah's Critical Concepts Question the assumptions of your CCs Fails if...
1. I am able to afford it How much does each college cost? Show Nylah's Answer
I can’t accurately figure out the total costs. What can my family afford?
2. The school has a good graphic design program What makes a good graphic design program? Show Nylah's Answer
I don’t research schools’ design courses and job placement data
3. I still want to help out at home What kind of help does my home need from me? Show Nylah's Answer
I am too far from home to help out on a regular basis
