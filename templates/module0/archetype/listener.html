    • {% endblock %} {% block blind_spot_text %} Being a Listener means that you may rely too heavily on other people to make decisions for you. Because you are loyal to those you trust, you can have a blind spot and make decisions based on what—or who!— you like, rather than on what's actually best. This is called the "liking bias." In addition because you like to listen to others, hearing their stories may lead you to the “narrative bias,” where you overvalue storylines and undervalue data. When you pay too much attention to what others are thinking and doing, you stop thinking for yourself. This "social proof bias" means that you just do what others recommend, instead of thinking about what you actually want. {% endblock %} {% block blind_spot_list %}
  • Liking Bias: Tendency to overlook the faults of, and complies with, wishes of well-liked others
  • Narrative Fallacy: Tendency to make sense of the world through stories
  • Social Proof Bias: Tendency to adopt the actions of others in an attempt to reflect correct behavior in a given situation
