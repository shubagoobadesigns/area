    • {% endblock %} {% block blind_spot_text %} Being the Visionary does have its drawbacks. You have a tendency to believe that with a creative spirit, you can accomplish anything. This can lead to "optimism bias" where you are over confident in your judgment. Optimism can also lead to "planning fallacy" where you think making a decision will take you less time to make than it actually does. This means that you can fall behind. Because you tend to lean on your imagination, rather than do research, your opinions are sometimes shaped by limited information. Because you are drawn to exciting ideas, you often get attached to the most recent or bold information or ideas, even if they aren't ultimately the most important. This "saliency bias" leads you to make you make judgements based on a superficial understanding of the decision you're trying to make. Finally, because you like to go our own way, you sometimes try too hard to be an original. This tendency to overvalue originality because it's rare, called the "scarcity bias," leads you invest in being different as opposed to making the smartest decision. {% endblock %} {% block blind_spot_list %}
  • Loss Aversion: Tendency to prefer avoiding losses to acquiring equivalent gains: it is better to not lose $5 than to find $5
  • Relativity Bias: Tendency to make relative, rather than absolute evaluations
  • Authority Bias: Tendency to attribute greater accuracy to the opinion of an authority figure
