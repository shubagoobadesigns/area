You are... The Adventurer!

You make decisions quickly and you trust your gut feeling. When faced with a challenge, big or small, you’d rather do what feels right than spend your time thinking through all the choices. You know who you are, and you know what you want—so you aren’t afraid to go get it.

Strengths

Being The adventurer comes with a lot of perks. One of your defining strengths is your confidence—you believe in yourself, and you know what you stand for. That makes it easy for you to judge if a decision is the right one for you. That’s a powerful thing! This confidence probably shows up in your life in a lot of ways, but its most obvious when you’ve got a big decision ahead of you. Another defining strength is that you are built for speed—you move quickly! You’re not afraid to go boldly toward what you want, and fast. That lets you get a lot done in a short period of time.

Blind Spots

There are some downfalls to this decision-making style, too. Sometimes, your confidence makes you believe that things are going to go your way—and sometimes, they won't. This "optimism bias" makes you feel unstoppable, and sometimes, that can lead you into danger. Because you move quickly, you sometimes don’t always have an accurate sense for how long something really takes, and so this "planning fallacy" makes you run the risk of being behind and not actually accomplishing everything you wanted to. And finally, because have such strong beliefs in what is right and what is wrong, you aren't always able to look at the full picture. Sometimes, you'll see evidence that tells you that you’re going in the wrong direction, but your "confirmation bias" leads you to find a way to make it support what you think is right. Watch out for these biases that you have—if you know what they are, you can make sure to protect yourself from them!
