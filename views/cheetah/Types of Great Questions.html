You may already know people who might be able to help you with your decision. But what should ask them to make sure that you get answers that are useful? To figure that out consider that there are four major kinds of questions that are commonly asked and each kind will get you different answers. Read about them and think about: What kinds of answers will be most useful to me in making my decision?

The four major kinds of questions are:

  1. Behavior Questions - what someone does or has done. These kinds of questions will give you answers that tell you about actual experiences, activities, and actions.
  2. Opinion Questions - what someone thinks about a person, place or thing. These questions will give you answers that shed light on other people's goals, intentions, desires and values.
  3. Feeling Questions - how someone responds emotionally to a topic. These questions will give you answers about others' emotional responses to their experiences and thoughts.
  4. Knowledge Questions - to find out what factual information other people have. These questions will give you answers that have evidence and facts about the decision you need to make.

The goal of making you aware of different types of questions is to help you use your time wisely when you decide to reach out to other people about your decision. By knowing the four main types of questions you can better think about what you really need to know and then ask the right question to the right person to get the answers you need to make an informed decision.
